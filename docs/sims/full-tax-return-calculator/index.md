## Overview

This is the most comprehensive simulator in the book — a guided walkthrough of the entire Form 1040 calculation, broken into logical steps. It mirrors the actual return sequence: filing status and personal information, income from all sources, above-the-line adjustments, deduction choice, taxable income, tax computation, credits, and finally the payments and refund/balance-due calculation.

Because it covers all the moving parts together, it's the best tool for understanding how different elements interact. Add a side gig and watch SE tax appear. Make an IRA contribution and see AGI drop. Add children and watch the Child Tax Credit cut your liability. This calculator is for learning how everything fits together, not for filing an actual return.

## How to Use

The calculator is organized as a multi-step wizard — work through each step in order:

1. **Filing Status & Personal Info** — status, age, dependency status
2. **Income** — W-2 wages, self-employment, interest, dividends, capital gains, rental income, other
3. **Adjustments to Income** — IRA, HSA, SE health insurance, SE tax deduction, student loan interest
4. **Deduction** — standard vs. itemized comparison, with full itemized entry form
5. **Tax Computation** — bracket-by-bracket calculation on ordinary income; separate LTCG rate computation
6. **Credits** — Child Tax Credit, Child & Dependent Care, Education Credits, EITC (if eligible)
7. **Other Taxes** — SE tax, AMT (simplified), net investment income tax
8. **Payments** — withholding from W-2s, estimated tax payments, refundable credits
9. **Summary** — total refund or amount owed, with full line-by-line breakdown

## Key Concepts

This calculator integrates all major tax concepts covered in the book:

- Filing status determines brackets, standard deduction, and credit phase-outs
- AGI is the gateway number — it determines eligibility for many deductions and credits
- Self-employment income triggers SE tax (15.3%) and allows the SE deduction (50% of SE tax)
- The standard deduction benefits most filers; itemizing requires Schedule A documentation
- Credits reduce tax dollar-for-dollar; refundable credits can produce a refund beyond zero liability
- LTCG and qualified dividends are taxed at preferential 0%/15%/20% rates

!!! note "For Educational Purposes"
    This calculator is a learning tool. It simplifies some phase-outs and does not cover every tax situation. For actual filing, use IRS-approved software or consult a tax professional.
