## Overview

The Traditional vs. Roth decision comes down to one question: will you pay a lower tax rate now or in retirement? A Traditional IRA gives you a deduction today but you pay ordinary income tax on withdrawals. A Roth IRA uses after-tax dollars now, but all qualified withdrawals — including decades of growth — come out tax-free.

This calculator projects the after-tax value of the same contribution amount in both account types over your chosen investment horizon, using your current and expected retirement tax brackets. It shows the crossover point: the bracket at which Roth starts to win.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your current tax bracket** — the marginal rate you pay on the last dollar of income today
2. **Enter your expected retirement bracket** — what you expect to pay on withdrawals in retirement
3. **Set contribution amount** — up to the 2025 limit of $7,000 ($8,000 if age 50+)
4. **Set years to retirement and expected annual return**
5. **Read the summary table** — it shows pre-tax and after-tax values for both account types

## Key Concepts

- 2025 IRA contribution limit: $7,000 / $8,000 (age 50+)
- Traditional IRA: deductible contributions, taxable withdrawals
- Roth IRA: after-tax contributions, tax-free qualified withdrawals
- If current bracket < retirement bracket → Roth usually wins
- Income limits apply for Roth eligibility and Traditional deductibility
