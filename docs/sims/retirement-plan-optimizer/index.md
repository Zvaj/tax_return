## Overview

Maxing out tax-advantaged accounts is the single most powerful tax strategy available to most Americans. But the contribution limits, eligibility rules, and income phase-outs across 401(k), Traditional IRA, Roth IRA, HSA, and SEP-IRA are easy to confuse. This optimizer lays out exactly how much you can contribute to each account type given your income, age, and employment situation — and shows the total tax savings for each dollar contributed.

For self-employed filers it also calculates SEP-IRA and Solo 401(k) limits, which are based on a percentage of net self-employment income.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your income** — W-2 wages and/or net self-employment income
2. **Select your age** — catch-up contributions apply at 50+ (and special 60–63 limits for 401k)
3. **Indicate employer plan access** — 401(k), 403(b), SIMPLE IRA, etc.
4. **Enter HSA eligibility** — self-only or family HDHP coverage
5. **Review the contribution table** — it shows the max for each account and the combined tax reduction at your bracket

## Key Concepts

- 2025 401(k) limit: $23,500 / $31,000 (50+) / $34,750 (ages 60–63)
- 2025 IRA limit: $7,000 / $8,000 (50+)
- 2025 HSA: $4,300 self-only / $8,550 family
- SEP-IRA: up to 25% of net SE income, max $70,000
- Roth IRA phase-out: $150,000–$165,000 (Single), $236,000–$246,000 (MFJ)
