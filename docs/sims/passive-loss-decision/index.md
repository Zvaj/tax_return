## Overview

Rental property losses are "passive" under IRS rules — meaning you generally can't deduct them against W-2 wages or other ordinary income. But there are two major exceptions: the $25,000 passive loss allowance for active participants (phases out between $100k–$150k MAGI), and the real estate professional exception for those who spend more than 750 hours per year in real estate activities.

This decision tree guides you through the key questions in order — do you actively participate? What is your MAGI? Do you qualify as a real estate professional? — and tells you exactly how much loss you can deduct now versus how much carries forward to future years.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your rental loss** for the year (from Schedule E)
2. **Enter your MAGI** — modified adjusted gross income (before the rental loss)
3. **Answer the participation questions** — do you actively manage the property? How many hours did you spend on real estate activities?
4. **Read the decision output** — it shows deductible loss this year, carried-forward loss, and the rule that applies

## Key Concepts

- Passive activity loss (PAL) rules: IRC §469
- $25,000 rental loss allowance: phases out $1 for every $2 of MAGI over $100,000
- Real estate professional: 750+ hours AND more than half of all work time in real estate
- Suspended losses carry forward indefinitely and are released when the property is sold
- Active participation (less stringent than RE professional) is needed for the $25k allowance
