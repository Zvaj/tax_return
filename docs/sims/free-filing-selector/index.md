## Overview

Most Americans can file their federal taxes for free — but navigating the options is confusing. IRS Free File covers filers under $84,000 AGI through brand-name software partners. IRS Direct File (launched 2024) lets eligible filers prepare and submit directly on the IRS website at no cost. VITA (Volunteer Income Tax Assistance) offers free in-person help for those earning under $67,000. And some commercial software offers genuinely free tiers for simple returns.

This selector asks about your income, age, military status, state, and return complexity to match you with the best option — or combination of options — for your situation.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your approximate AGI** for 2025
2. **Answer situational questions** — military, age 60+, limited English, disabilities, self-employed, rental income, etc.
3. **Select your state** — Direct File availability and state partnership vary
4. **View your matched options** — ranked by best fit, with links and eligibility notes for each program

## Key Concepts

- IRS Free File: available for AGI under $84,000; partner software may upsell state returns
- IRS Direct File: free, no third-party software; available in ~25 states for simpler returns
- VITA/TCE: free in-person preparation for income-eligible filers and seniors
- AARP Tax-Aide: TCE variant for taxpayers 50+, no income limit
- MilTax: free filing for active duty military and veterans regardless of income
