## Overview

If you use your vehicle for business, you can deduct the cost two ways: the standard mileage rate (70¢/mile for 2025) or actual expenses (gas, insurance, repairs, depreciation) prorated by business-use percentage. The standard mileage method is simpler; the actual expense method sometimes yields a larger deduction, especially for expensive or gas-heavy vehicles.

There are important restrictions: you must choose the standard mileage method in the first year the vehicle is placed in service if you want to use it in later years. And if you use actual expenses with depreciation, MACRS depreciation rules and luxury auto limits apply.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your total annual business miles** and total miles driven
2. **Enter your standard mileage rate deduction** — automatically calculated at 70¢/mile
3. **For actual expenses**: enter gas, insurance, registration, repairs/maintenance, and lease payments or depreciation basis
4. **Compare the two columns** — the calculator shows total deduction under each method and the business-use percentage applied to actual expenses
5. **Note the first-year election rule** — if this is a new vehicle, your choice this year locks in your method going forward

## Key Concepts

- 2025 standard mileage rate: 70 cents per mile (business)
- Actual expense method: all vehicle costs × (business miles ÷ total miles)
- Luxury auto depreciation caps apply to vehicles over ~$12,400 cost basis
- Section 179 and bonus depreciation (40% in 2025) can accelerate depreciation under actual method
- Mileage log is required for both methods — date, destination, business purpose, miles
