## Overview

This calculator covers the most common tax situations for individual filers in 2025. Enter W-2 wages, freelance/self-employment income, and other income sources, then apply above-the-line deductions (HSA, Traditional IRA, student loan interest) to arrive at your AGI. The calculator then compares your itemized deductions against the standard deduction and applies the correct tax brackets.

Self-employment income gets special treatment: the calculator automatically computes the 15.3% SE tax on 92.35% of net SE income, then deducts half of that SE tax above the line — exactly as IRS rules require.

Use this as a sanity-check before filing or when evaluating whether a deduction strategy is worth pursuing.

## How to Use

1. **Select your filing status** — Single, MFJ, MFS, or Head of Household
2. **Enter your income** — W-2 wages, freelance net income, and any other taxable income
3. **Choose HSA coverage** and enter HSA/IRA/student-loan contributions
4. **Itemized deductions** — enter mortgage interest, property tax, state income tax, and charitable gifts to see if itemizing beats the standard deduction
5. **Review the right panel** — it shows AGI, taxable income, bracket-by-bracket tax, SE tax, and estimated refund or amount owed

## Key Concepts

- Standard deduction (2025): $15,000 Single / $30,000 MFJ
- SE tax rate: 15.3% on net income × 0.9235
- Above-the-line deductions reduce AGI before choosing standard vs. itemized
- SALT deduction cap: $10,000
