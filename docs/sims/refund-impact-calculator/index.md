## Overview

Your tax refund (or bill) is not determined by your income alone — it is the difference between your total tax liability and the taxes you already prepaid through withholding and estimated payments. This calculator makes that relationship interactive: adjust any input and watch the refund meter update immediately.

It's particularly useful for year-end planning. With a few months left in the year, you can model the impact of making an IRA contribution, increasing withholding on your W-4, or claiming a credit you forgot — and see exactly how it affects your bottom line before the tax year closes.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your filing status** and **wages/income sources**
2. **Enter federal tax withheld** from your W-2(s) and any estimated tax payments made
3. **Enter refundable credits** — EITC, Child Tax Credit (refundable portion), etc.
4. **Adjust above-the-line deductions** — IRA, HSA, student loan interest
5. **Choose standard or itemized deduction** and enter itemized totals if applicable
6. **Watch the refund/owed display** update — green means refund, red means you owe

## Key Concepts

- Refund = taxes prepaid − taxes owed (when prepaid > owed)
- Balance due = taxes owed − taxes prepaid (when owed > prepaid)
- A large refund means you over-withheld — essentially an interest-free loan to the IRS
- Adjust W-4 withholding to target a near-zero result at filing
- Refundable credits (EITC, ACTC) can produce a refund even if you owe no tax
