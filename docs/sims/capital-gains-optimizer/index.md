## Overview

Long-term capital gains enjoy preferential tax rates — 0%, 15%, or 20% — that are completely separate from ordinary income brackets. The rate you pay depends on your *total* taxable income, not just the gain itself. This can create a planning opportunity: if your ordinary income is low enough, you may be able to realize gains at 0%.

This optimizer lets you enter your ordinary income and investment gains side-by-side and shows exactly which rate applies to each dollar of gain, where the thresholds fall, and how much additional gain you could realize before crossing into the next bracket.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your filing status** and **ordinary taxable income** (wages, SE income, etc. after deductions)
2. **Enter your long-term capital gains** and/or qualified dividend amount
3. **View the bracket visualization** — a stacked bar or chart shows ordinary income vs. gains and which LTCG rate bucket each dollar falls into
4. **Adjust the gains slider** to find the maximum gain you can take at 0% this year

## Key Concepts

- 2025 LTCG 0% threshold: $48,350 (Single) / $96,700 (MFJ)
- 2025 LTCG 15% threshold: up to $533,400 (Single) / $600,050 (MFJ)
- 20% rate applies above those thresholds
- LTCG "stacks on top of" ordinary income when determining the rate
- Qualified dividends are taxed at the same preferential rates as LTCG
