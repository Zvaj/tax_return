## Overview

The redesigned W-4 (post-2020) replaced withholding allowances with a more direct system: you enter other income, deductions above the standard amount, and any extra amount to withhold per period. But many people still end up with a surprise bill or a larger refund than they wanted.

This tool works backward from your desired outcome. Tell it your filing status, expected total income from all jobs, any other income sources, and what you want at filing (refund target or near-zero). It calculates the exact dollar amounts to enter on Steps 3, 4a, 4b, and 4c of your W-4 — the instructions you hand to your employer.

## How to Use

1. **Enter your filing status** and **pay frequency** (weekly, biweekly, monthly, etc.)
2. **Enter expected annual income** from this job and any other jobs or income sources
3. **Enter dependents** — the Child Tax Credit amount from Step 3
4. **Set your target outcome** — desired refund amount or "as close to zero as possible"
5. **Read the W-4 entries** — the tool outputs exactly what to write on Steps 4a, 4b, and 4c

## Key Concepts

- Step 2: check the box if you have multiple jobs or a working spouse
- Step 3: enter total Child Tax Credit ($2,000 per qualifying child under 17)
- Step 4a: other income not subject to withholding (interest, dividends, side income)
- Step 4b: extra deductions above the standard deduction (if you itemize)
- Step 4c: additional flat dollar amount to withhold per pay period
- Update your W-4 whenever you have a major life change (marriage, new job, child, etc.)
