## Overview

When you work for yourself, no employer withholds taxes from your paycheck. The IRS instead requires you to prepay taxes in four installments throughout the year. Getting this wrong in either direction costs you: underpay and you owe a penalty at filing; overpay and you've given the government an interest-free loan.

This calculator takes your expected gross business income, deductible expenses, filing status, and prior-year tax (for the safe-harbor calculation) and produces the recommended payment for each quarter. It also factors in the self-employed health insurance deduction, which lowers both your income tax and SE tax base.

## How to Use

1. **Enter annual gross business income** — your expected total revenue for 2025
2. **Enter annual business expenses** — deductible costs to arrive at net profit
3. **Select filing status** and check if you have additional W-2 income
4. **Enter prior-year total tax** — used for the 100%/110% safe-harbor option
5. **Enter self-employed health insurance premiums** if applicable
6. **Review quarterly amounts** — the calculator shows the recommended payment for each of the four due dates

## Key Concepts

- SE tax: 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare) on 92.35% of net SE income
- 2025 due dates: April 15, June 16, September 15, January 15, 2026
- Safe harbor: pay 100% of prior-year tax (110% if prior AGI > $150k) to avoid penalties
- Self-employed health insurance premiums are deductible above the line
