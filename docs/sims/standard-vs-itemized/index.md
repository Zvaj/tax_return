## Overview

Most Americans take the standard deduction because the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act roughly doubled it in 2018. But homeowners with large mortgages or high charitable giving may still benefit from itemizing. This calculator makes the comparison concrete: enter your mortgage interest, property taxes, state income taxes, charitable contributions, and qualifying medical expenses, and it shows your itemized total side-by-side with the standard deduction for your filing status.

The calculator also applies the SALT (state and local tax) cap of $10,000, the AGI floor for medical deductions (7.5% of AGI in 2025), and flags which method saves more.

## How to Use

1. **Select your filing status** to see the applicable standard deduction
2. **Enter your AGI** — needed to calculate the medical expense floor
3. **Fill in your potential itemized deductions** — mortgage interest, property tax, state income tax, charitable gifts, and out-of-pocket medical expenses
4. **Read the verdict** — the calculator shows your total itemized amount vs. the standard deduction and recommends the larger one

## Key Concepts

- 2025 standard deductions: $15,000 (Single), $30,000 (MFJ), $22,500 (HoH)
- SALT cap: combined state/local income and property taxes capped at $10,000
- Medical deductions: only the amount exceeding 7.5% of AGI is deductible
- You can only itemize on Schedule A if you forgo the standard deduction
