## Overview

Owning rental property involves a tax calculation that's more complex than most realize. Gross rent is only the starting point — you subtract operating expenses, then depreciation (a non-cash deduction based on 1/27.5 of the building's cost basis), to arrive at net taxable income or a net loss. If you end up with a loss, the passive activity rules determine how much you can deduct now.

This calculator walks through the full Schedule E computation: gross rents, operating expenses (mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, repairs, management fees), depreciation, and the resulting net income or loss. It then applies the passive loss allowance rules based on your MAGI and participation level.

## How to Use

1. **Enter gross rental income** for the year
2. **Enter all operating expenses** — mortgage interest, taxes, insurance, repairs, utilities, management fees, other
3. **Enter the property's cost basis** (purchase price minus land value) to compute depreciation
4. **Enter your MAGI** and participation type to determine deductibility of any loss
5. **Review the Schedule E summary** — it shows each line item, net income/loss, deductible vs. suspended loss

## Key Concepts

- Depreciation: residential rental property depreciated over 27.5 years (straight-line)
- Net rental loss = passive loss (subject to PAL rules)
- $25,000 allowance phases out at MAGI $100k–$150k for active participants
- Mortgage interest on rental is fully deductible (no SALT cap applies to rental property)
- Repairs are deductible; improvements must be capitalized and depreciated
