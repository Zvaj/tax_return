# Course Description

## Course Title

**Tax Return Guide: A Plain-English Guide to Filing, Saving, and Winning at Taxes (2025)**

---

## Course Overview

This is a 12-chapter intelligent textbook for the general public covering the complete US federal and state tax system. Unlike most tax resources that are either too vague or written for professionals, this guide uses plain English, real dollar amounts, and practical examples to walk any adult through filing their own taxes, legally minimizing what they owe, and building smart tax habits year-round.

The book covers W-2 employees, 1099 contractors, side hustlers, investors, rental property owners, and anyone who wants to stop overpaying or underpaying the IRS.

---

## Writing Tone and Style

- **Voice:** A knowledgeable friend who is a CPA — someone who gives you straight answers, not hedged non-answers driven by liability
- **Language:** Plain English, no jargon without explanation
- **Numbers:** Real dollar amounts in every example, not vague percentages
- **Action-oriented:** Each chapter ends with a "What To Do Right Now" section
- **Non-judgmental:** Whether you owe money or are getting a refund, no shame — just actionable steps

---

## Target Audience

- **Primary:** General public adults filing US taxes
- **Includes:** First-time filers, W-2 employees, 1099 contractors, side hustlers, gig workers, investors, landlords, and anyone who wants to pay less in taxes legally
- **Age range:** 18 and older
- **Prior knowledge required:** Basic arithmetic and reading comprehension — no tax or financial background needed

---

## Prerequisites

- Basic arithmetic (addition, subtraction, percentages)
- Ability to read a form or document
- No prior tax, finance, or accounting knowledge required

---

## The 12 Chapters

1. **Understanding Your Tax Situation** — Filing status, income types, taxable vs non-taxable income, how the IRS views your financial life
2. **Filing Your Return Step by Step** — Standard vs itemized deductions, tax credits, how to actually file, free filing options
3. **Legal Tax Reduction Strategies** — Retirement contributions, HSA, education credits, charitable giving, and other legal ways to reduce your tax bill
4. **Side Hustle & Self-Employment Taxes** — SE tax calculation, quarterly estimated payments, deductible business expenses, Schedule C
5. **Year-Round Tax Planning** — W-4 withholding optimization, tax-loss harvesting, Roth conversions, staying ahead of April
6. **Tax Forms Deep Dive** — Every box on the W-2, 1099-NEC, 1099-INT, 1099-DIV, and 1040 explained in plain English
7. **Investments & Capital Gains Taxes** — Short-term vs long-term capital gains rates, dividend taxation, cryptocurrency, wash sale rule
8. **Real Estate & Rental Property Taxes** — Depreciation, rental income and expense deductions, selling your primary residence ($250k/$500k exclusion)
9. **Advanced Strategies & Loopholes** — Backdoor Roth IRA, Augusta Rule, Solo 401(k), Dependent Care FSA, health insurance deduction for self-employed, energy credits
10. **State Taxes Overview** — How state income taxes work, the 9 no-income-tax states, multi-state filing, state-specific deductions
11. **IRS Audits & How to Avoid Them** — Common audit triggers, what the IRS looks for, how to respond if selected, record-keeping best practices
12. **Free Filing Resources & Tools** — IRS Free File, VITA program, IRS Direct File, best free tax software, and when to hire a professional

## Learning Objectives by Bloom's Taxonomy

### Remember
- Recall the 2025 federal tax brackets and standard deduction amounts
- Name the most common tax forms (W-2, 1099, 1040, Schedule C)
- Identify the four filing statuses and their eligibility requirements

### Understand
- Explain the difference between a tax deduction and a tax credit
- Describe how marginal tax brackets work (and bust the "higher bracket" myth)
- Explain what self-employment tax is and who pays it

### Apply
- Calculate your taxable income after standard deduction
- Determine whether to take the standard deduction or itemize
- Complete a Schedule C for basic self-employment income
- Calculate quarterly estimated tax payments

### Analyze
- Compare the after-tax cost of traditional vs Roth retirement contributions
- Analyze whether a side hustle business deduction is legitimate vs risky
- Evaluate which tax-advantaged accounts best match your situation

### Evaluate
- Assess which legal tax reduction strategies apply to your specific situation
- Judge when to file yourself vs hire a tax professional
- Evaluate the audit risk of specific deductions before claiming them

### Create
- Build a personalized year-round tax calendar
- Design a record-keeping system for business expenses
- Create a tax projection to estimate your refund or balance due before April

## Topics Not Covered

- Advanced corporate tax law (C-corps, S-corps)
- International tax (FBAR, FATCA, foreign income exclusion)
- Estate and gift tax (beyond basic overview)
- Tax court procedures and legal disputes
- Specific professional advice — this book is educational, not a substitute for a CPA

## Expected Outcomes

After completing this guide, you will be able to:

- [ ] File your own federal and state tax return using free tools
- [ ] Calculate how much you owe or your expected refund before filing
- [ ] Identify at least 3 legal deductions or credits you were not previously claiming
- [ ] Set up a quarterly estimated tax system if you have self-employment income
- [ ] Understand every line item on your W-2 and 1099 forms
- [ ] Know exactly which records to keep and for how long
- [ ] Have a year-round tax plan so April is never a surprise
