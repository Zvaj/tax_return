# Chapters

This textbook is organized into 12 chapters covering 200 concepts across every aspect of US federal and state taxes.

## Chapter Overview

1. [Understanding Your Tax Situation](01-understanding-taxes/index.md) — Foundations of the US tax system: income types, AGI, tax brackets, filing statuses, and how your tax liability is calculated.
2. [Filing Your Return Step by Step](02-filing-your-return/index.md) — How to actually file: Form 1040, deductions vs. credits, withholding, refunds, extensions, and key credits (Child, EITC, education).
3. [Legal Tax Reduction Strategies](03-tax-reduction-strategies/index.md) — Retirement accounts (IRA, 401k, HSA, FSA), itemized deductions, energy credits, charitable giving, and above-the-line deductions.
4. [Side Hustle & Self-Employment Taxes](04-side-hustle-taxes/index.md) — SE tax, Schedule C, business deductions, home office, quarterly estimated taxes, QBI, and self-employed retirement accounts.
5. [Year-Round Tax Planning](05-year-round-planning/index.md) — W-4 optimization, withholding adjustments, Roth conversions, bunching deductions, tax bracket management, and building your tax calendar.
6. [Tax Forms Deep Dive](06-tax-forms-deep-dive/index.md) — Every box on the W-2, 1099 variants (NEC, INT, R, G, K), the 1040 line-by-line, Schedule A/SE, and retirement penalty forms.
7. [Investments & Capital Gains Taxes](07-investments-capital-gains/index.md) — Capital gains rates, dividends, cryptocurrency, wash sale rule, cost basis methods, mutual funds, ETFs, stock options, and tax-loss harvesting.
8. [Real Estate & Rental Property Taxes](08-real-estate-rental-taxes/index.md) — Rental income, depreciation, passive loss rules, primary residence exclusion, 1031 exchanges, and rental-specific tax forms.
9. [Advanced Strategies & Loopholes](09-advanced-strategies/index.md) — Backdoor Roth, Augusta Rule, S-Corp election, donor-advised funds, NOL, cost segregation, bonus depreciation, and Section 179.
10. [State Taxes Overview](10-state-taxes/index.md) — State income tax structures, no-income-tax states, multi-state filing, domicile rules, reciprocity agreements, and remote worker rules.
11. [IRS Audits & How to Avoid Them](11-irs-audits/index.md) — Audit types and triggers, documentation, record retention, CP2000 notices, penalty abatement, and offers in compromise.
12. [Free Filing Resources & Tools](12-free-filing-resources/index.md) — IRS Free File, Direct File, VITA, AARP Tax Aide, free software options, and when to hire a tax professional.

## How to Use This Textbook

Each chapter builds on the ones before it — concepts are introduced only after their prerequisites have been covered. If you are new to taxes, start at Chapter 1 and work forward. If you have a specific goal, you can jump ahead, but Chapters 1–3 provide the vocabulary you need for everything else. Every chapter ends with a **"What To Do Right Now"** action section.

!!! tip "Where to start"

    - **First-time filer?** → Start with [Chapter 1](01-understanding-taxes/index.md)
    - **Need to file right now?** → Jump to [Chapter 2](02-filing-your-return/index.md)
    - **Have a side hustle?** → Start with [Chapter 4](04-side-hustle-taxes/index.md)
    - **Want to pay less in taxes?** → Start with [Chapter 3](03-tax-reduction-strategies/index.md)

---

**Note:** Each chapter lists all concepts covered. Dependencies are respected throughout — no concept appears before it has been properly introduced.
